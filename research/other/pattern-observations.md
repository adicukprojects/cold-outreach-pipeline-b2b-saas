# Pattern Observations Across the 10 Experts

A few cross-cutting patterns noticed while collecting this research, worth keeping in mind when building the eventual playbook.

## 1. Two distinct content styles emerged

Most experts split into two camps:

- **Framework-first practitioners** (Jason Bay, Jeremy Chatelaine, Sam Nelson): publish named, repeatable methodologies with stated results (e.g. Jason Bay's "Trigger/Tension/Trust" framework, Sam Nelson's Agoge Sequence, Jeremy Chatelaine's 11-lesson cold email framework).
- **Live-practice demonstrators** (Will Allred, Josh Braun, Kyle Coleman): post real, specific examples they personally encountered — actual emails received, actual calls made — and react to them in real time.

Both are valuable, but they serve different purposes in a playbook: framework-first content gives you the "system," live-practice content gives you the "judgment" for applying it.

## 2. Engagement-bait CTAs appear in a minority of posts

A few posts (Conrad Niedzielski, Alex Vacca) end with "comment X to get the guide" or "repost this" calls to action. The core content in these posts was still substantive enough to include, but this is a distinct pattern from genuine teaching — worth filtering for intent when reusing this material, since the CTA pattern is itself a (legitimate) cold-outreach/audience-growth tactic, separate from the cold email/LinkedIn outreach tactics being taught.

## 3. Some experts' current content has drifted from their original niche

Florin Tatulea's recent LinkedIn activity leans toward AI/GTM strategy rather than pure cold-email copywriting, and Sam Nelson's recent activity is mostly conference/community-building rather than tactical content. Both are still legitimate, currently-active practitioners, but it's a reminder that "expert in X" doesn't guarantee their most recent public content is about X — sometimes the strongest material is their older, foundational work (as with Sam Nelson's 2018 Agoge Referral Request article, included here for that reason).

## 4. AI is now a recurring theme across multiple experts

Independently, Florin Tatulea, Alex Vacca, and Kyle Coleman all posted about AI's role in outbound (data quality for AI tools, AI-assisted prompt libraries, AI vs. human SDRs) within the same few-month window. This wasn't something I searched for — it emerged naturally across separate experts, suggesting AI-augmented outbound is a live, current topic worth its own section in any future playbook.
