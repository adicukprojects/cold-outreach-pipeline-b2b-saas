We replaced most of our outbound busywork with AI agents this year.

Every job an SDR used to do manually now has a tool for it. Here's the full menu.

You don't need all 24. The point is knowing what can be handed off, so your team spends its hours where they move the pipeline.

Finding the right accounts
✅ Clone your best customers into lookalike lists (Clay, OpenMart)
✅ Turn public company data into pain-point briefs (Relevance AI)
✅ Score every account for fit, no manual rules (Apollo)
✅ Fill in missing titles, phones, and tech stacks (FullEnrich, Wiza, Prospeo)

Catching the moment
✅ Watch for job changes and role moves (PredictLeads)
✅ Read website visits and content signals for intent (Common Room)
✅ Fire the sequence the second intent spikes (Instantly)
✅ Flag when a competitor hires, launches, or raises (Common Room)

Writing the message
✅ Draft openers that reference something real about each prospect (Clay, Relevance AI)
✅ Shift tone by persona and channel (Relevance AI)
✅ Write follow-ups that pick up where the last reply left off (Artisan, Lemlist)
✅ Test subject lines without building A/B rules (Instantly)

Landing in the inbox
✅ Warm up senders and build reputation before you send (Instantly)
✅ Rotate inboxes to protect deliverability (Lemlist)
✅ Run LinkedIn requests and DMs at scale (Expandi)
✅ Catch spam triggers before a campaign tanks (Instantly)

Handling replies
✅ Sort replies by intent: interested, objection, not now (Artisan)
✅ Draft a reply that fits the thread in seconds (Lemlist)
✅ Book the meeting from reply to calendar, hands-off (Nooks)
✅ Route hot leads to the right rep in real time (Attio)

Getting smarter each cycle
✅ Surface the copy that's landing (Instantly)
✅ Learn who really converts and tighten the ICP (Apollo)
✅ Score a campaign's odds before you launch it (Relevance AI)
✅ Tell you exactly what to test next (Relevance AI, Clay)

Start with the 2 or 3 jobs eating the most hours. Add the rest only when they earn it.

That's where outbound is now.

Save this as your build checklist.
