I've been working w a founder recently that's given me some really good perspective on startup "pace".

The problem with pacing yourself is purely psychological. Any downtime feels guilty. But, that's a fast way to find burnout and rob yourself of critical reflection time.

Reflection time can't be optimized for until you know the plan, the milestones, and the timeline.

"No meeting Wednesdays" are performative without proper guidance.

I've always loved the Okta founder quote: "Startups are not a sprint... but the marathon is run at a 7 minute pace".

If you're running a 7 min pace... you can't afford to go off course. Ultra marathons add to the "suck".

Let's say you need to enter a new market.

You need 5-10 solid POCs to validate the market.

It's easy to start to get ahead of the work. Building campaigns, thinking about the scaled sales org etc.

Sit with it. Don't act.

F***ing chill.

You haven't validated the market yet. You're going to run an extra mile in your marathon.

Back to the Ex. Let's say you get a few POCs via referrals...

"Oh, lets hire sellers"... well... did you validate that? Do you have a plan? Do you know what the work is?

Slow down. Reflect. Plan. Act to the plan.

Founder energy is different... I get it. I average 5:30 hours of sleep a night. But side quests are dangerous. The well is finite.

PS. One thing I've seen with this founder that's helped them (and I'm working on myself) is finding a personal rhythm. A weekly rhythm of sprints and rests. I'll be working on my own these next few months. First thing I've found - carving out a block to call people and working in a coffee shop are both net positives for creativity.
