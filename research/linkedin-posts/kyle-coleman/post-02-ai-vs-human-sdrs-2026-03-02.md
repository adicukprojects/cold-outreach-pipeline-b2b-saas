A big player in our space just cut their 100 person inbound SDR team. My reaction?

😏 More pipeline for us!

A wild number of people (who have never done SDR work before) believe that if a person clicks "contact me" they'll always book next steps.

In my years doing this, I'd say the number of people who operate that way is under 20%.

Which leaves 80% who need to be engaged further. Educated more. Sold more. And wayyyy more often than not, that happens on the phone. Not via email, and not via online chat.

Which is to say… it needs to be done by humans.

So, we'll continue (efficiently) growing our SDR team. We'll continue investing in the human experience. We'll continue to be omni-channel and to maximize our lead conversion rate.

I have a hunch we'll scoop up some demand that slips through the cracks of the other company's AI-only process.

–––

At the same time… we'll continue to invest in AI SDR capabilities.

There's a very important time and a place for AI in the sales cycle. We're well aware, and we've integrated it nicely. If a prospect wants to engage immediately at 2:00am with an AI bot to book a meeting, they can!

For us, it's not "humans OR AI" it's "humans AND AI."

–––

PS. To be clear, this company didn't lay off 100 SDRs. They pivoted them to an outbound motion.
