A lot of people have been asking me for guidance on making a Non-Call Sequence as seen in The Agoge Playbook (Agoge pronounced: Uh-Go-Jee). A couple months ago we introduced a new Non-Call Sequence in our Agoge called The Agoge Referral Request and it has become wildly successful. Our response rate has increased more than 2X over our previous strategy and the number of opportunities generated have gone through the ceiling. I'm excited to share it!

First of all, "Non-Call Sequence" is a little bit of a misnomer. We do call these prospects, but only if they engage with our emails. The core sequence is email/social only but Outreach triggers a call for us as soon as it detects that an email has been opened 2+ times.

This type of sequence allows SDRs to reach out to prospects that otherwise wouldn't make sense to reach out to. The strategy here is to reach out to a large number of these lower priority prospects, and only call the most important ones. That way we don't take an inordinate amount of time away from our core strategy of calling decision makers through The Agoge Sequence.

The traditional "Non-Call" Sequence strategy is to send a more general message about your value proposition to a large number of prospects across departments. Occasionally someone will forward your email to the correct person or give you a referral. The response rates are low, but this strategy takes so little time that the return on investment for time spent is high.

The traditional approach works, but the Agoge Referral Request blows it out of the water and takes no more time.

The strategy of the Agoge Referral Request is to target only those directly below your target decision maker and focus the content on asking for a referral. This group doesn't receive many prospecting emails so it's easier to get their attention and they will give you a referral surprisingly often. You have a huge advantage because very few salespeople are doing this. This strategy wasn't even feasible before sales engagement platforms came along. It wasn't a good strategy a few years ago.

And that's why it works. It's a good idea that sounds like a bad idea.

Also, most salespeople don't realize how well referrals-up to the decision maker convert. If you use the referral sequence, you can convert about 1/3 of your referrals into opportunities once you get good at it.

We also adjusted The Agoge Playbook to focus 100% of our "non-call" sequence efforts on individuals that were most likely to refer us to our best entry point.

But what about the titles that are unclear?

There are some titles that just aren't clear. For us they are titles like "Sales Director" or "Sales VP." Someone with the title "Sales Director" is so rarely actually over a team that, on average, putting them in The Agoge Sequence is a poor use of time. It is wiser to put them into a non-call sequence.

So then what happens if you reach out to an unclear title with the Agoge Referral Request assuming they are an IC and they are actually a manager?

Awesome things! As it turns out, they are surprisingly likely to correct you. Now you've started a dialogue and their cell phone number is probably in their signature.

The best thing about this sequence is that it's very easy for a sales rep to implement it into their workflow. It takes a couple hours max to create and will yield extra new opportunities each month with very little extra work for the new reps. So implement it.
