No matter where you look, people seem to be obsessed with deliverability.

"Bro, if your prospects don't see your email, they will not respond", they say.

Hard to argue with this, and it sure sounds catchy, but if the goal is to get a response, I'd argue that you need to stop focusing on deliverability.

Your list is actually the most important element of your campaign.

I don't care how good your deliverability is, you won't get a response from someone who is not a good fit, whether you land in the inbox or not (see, I can make obvious arguments too).

Deliverability is simply the wrong lens to look at solving a lack of responses for cold email campaigns.

And focusing on it may be detrimental to your deliverability overall (sending rubbish emails to the wrong people will only get you more spam complaints).

Instead, you may get better results by approaching your campaign from a different angle.

Consider building your campaign like you would cook a good meal.

For example, if you want to make a pizza, you will need some dough.

Sure thing, but equally, you'll need to ensure people like eating pizzas.

And if you manage to find them, you'll also need to pay attention to the other ingredients, as the dough is not the pizza.

You can have a simple pizza (just tomato and cheese) or a deluxe one (with tons of things on top), it depends on what experience you want to have.

Both can be good, and both can be bad (but you always run the risk that one ingredient ruins the whole pizza).

So here is my recommendation (and yes, sure, I'm biased, but man, I worked more than 10 years on it to be the best):

Use QuickMail.com and take advantage of all the deliverability features we have consistently built in over the years (Deliverability AI, Reword with AI…) and focus on making a great campaign.

It's hard to go wrong when you start by focusing on your list, your value proposition, and your CTA.
