11 Years & billions of emails.

Here's what I've learned about cold outreach:

1. Mindset beats tactics:
Stop chasing hacks.
Cold email is about starting conversations, not dropping a booking link or forcing calls.

2. Strategy is everything:
Tactics without strategy are noise.
Start by asking:
- Why these people?
- Why now?
- Why should they care?

3. Volume ≠ strategy:
Sending more won't fix a broken campaign.
It just scales failure, and tanks your deliverability.

4. Your tools DO matter:
The wrong platform will quietly sabotage you.
Choose one that keeps up with ever-changing rules.

5. Cold outreach isn't just for sales:
It works for hiring, backlinks, podcast invites, partnerships...
Each use case needs a different playbook.

6. Small, focused campaigns win:
Especially when they're built on a smart angle and a solid follow-up plan.

7. List quality is everything:
There are 3 levels of prospecting.
Level 1: Basic firmographics: industry, company size, job title... (what everyone does)
Level 2: Behavioral signals: follows certain influencers, joins relevant groups, engages with specific topics...
Level 3: High-intent: Prospect is actively looking for what you offer. Hard to get, but game-changing when you do.
There are no good reasons to stay at level 1.

8. Channel fit matters:
Email, LinkedIn, phone... go where your prospect is active.
Multi-channel always outperforms single-channel.

9. Short, clear copy wins:
Ditch the info-dump.
Say just enough to spark curiosity and start a conversation.

10. CTAs shape your results:
Low-commitment CTAs boost replies.
High-commitment ones filter tire-kickers. Use both strategically.

11. Technical setup isn't optional:
SPF, DKIM, DMARC, inbox rotation, warming…
Skip these, and even a great strategy will fall flat.

Cold email still works.

You just have to do it right.
