250+ sales teams. 10,000+ reps. Here's the best cold call opener…👇

We've studied hundreds of thousands of cold calls across 250+ sales teams in the last 6 years. And there's one thing we can tell you for sure:

The best reps all use different openers. That's right. There's no one opener. Or handful of openers that work the best.

BUT…here's what every great opener that led to a meeting had in common.

The opener has three parts:

✅ Part 1) A greeting
"Hi [first name], it's [your first name…"
They answer the first question every prospect has: who are you?

✅ Part 2) The three Ts
Here's where it starts to get interesting. Each of the openers leverages one of what we call the "three Ts":
Trigger: Make it timely by finding signals/triggers
Tension: Make it relevant by sharing a problem
Trust: Make it credible by leveraging social proof

Sometimes they include all three. But every time, they include at least one.

"Hi [first name], it's [your first name…saw you're hiring welders…"
That's an example of leveraging a trigger.

✅ Part 3) Permission
Gong data from 300k+ cold calls supports the same. Permission-based openers outperform any other opener by nearly 5x.

"Hi [first name], it's [your first name…saw you're hiring welders…have a minute for me to share why I called you specifically?"
A simple permission-based question.

~~~

Now here's what it looks like when you start to get creative:

Greeting + Tension + Permission:
"Hi Dave, was calling about the welder shortage everyone's dealing with right now. Got a minute for me to share more?"

Greeting + Trigger + Tension + Trust + Permission:
"Hi Kate, noticed you're hiring MIG welders, not sure if you're running into challenges automating some of those low volume welds folks like Caterpillar have had. Got a min? Promise to be brief."

Greeting + Trust + Permission:
"Hi Tom, we work with several trailer hitch manufacturers like XYZ. It's Jason with ABC COMPANY. Heard our name tossed around?" (h/t to Armand Farrokh)

~~~

The best cold call openers are the ones you create. Trigger, tension, trust is just the ingredients. You make your own recipe.
